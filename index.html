About Natalie Archer

Natalie Archer obtained her B.A. in History from the University of Texas at Austin and her J.D. from Texas Wesleyan University School of Law, now Texas A&M School of Law. Natalie is actively involved in state and federal criminal defense. She started her career in federal bankruptcy cases but now focuses solely on criminal law.

Mrs. Archer grew up in Amarillo and graduated from Amarillo High School. After completing her post graduate education, she lived in Dallas, Texas and Chapel Hill, North Carolina before returning home to Amarillo where she has volunteered extensively for the Amarillo Independent School District.

Our Focus on Criminal Defense

At Archer Criminal Defense, we are passionate about defending your rights.

We know that being arrested and charged with a crime can be a terrifying, stressful experience that causes frustration and uncertainty for you and your family.

Criminal charges have serious consequences for your employment, reputation, and liberty.

Whatever charges you may be facing you want a defense attorney who is on your side and will vigilantly defend your rights. While I can never guarantee a particular outcome, I promise I will work diligently on your behalf.

We work across many areas of criminal law but focus on:

- DWI

- Assault

- Drug Posession or other Drug Charges

- Theft

- Solicitation

- Weapons Offenses / Firearms Offenses

- Felonies and Midemeanors

- Federal and State Crimes

Why It Matters

To defend your rights, you need an attorney that not only has the training and expertise but also the time to give your case the attention it deserves.

I pride myself on providing the time and attention it takes to get it right and get my clients the best outcome I can.

I don't just have the training and experience you want your defender to have - I also know how to listen and make sure I understand your side of the story.

You want and need a passionate attorney because a criminal conviction can result in: a jail or prison sentence, large monetary fines and fees, probation, loss of your driver's license, travel restrictions, difficulty with employers, or difficulty finding housing.
